Project Overview

ChillCabin is a provotype ‚Äî a deliberately evocative prototype ‚Äî that reimagines aviation UX through care rather than efficiency. Why This Project Exists

Modern airline apps are optimised for speed and transactions, but they rarely support emotional states in the moment. ChillCabin reframes the journey as a human experience: a passenger may be anxious, overstimulated, travelling alone, or simply needing reassurance. The project explores how future travel systems could respond with respect, privacy and autonomy.

What Makes It Speculative & Futuristic

ChillCabin assumes a near-future where emotional wellbeing is treated as part of passenger safety and service quality, and where inclusive design is mandatory. It speculates interfaces that prioritise calm, consent, and social choice ‚Äî including anonymity-by-design chat, fatigue-aware interactions and turbulence-reactive micro reassurance.

Ethics, Consent & Data Handling

The concept avoids emotional surveillance. Data is kept minimal and temporary (session-based), with clear consent surfaces embedded in the flow. Passengers can review, delete or exit without penalty. The system is designed to support ‚Äî not to diagnose, score or monetise feelings.

Research & Evaluation Approach

Evaluation focuses on plausibility and ethical robustness rather than performance metrics. Methods include heuristic evaluation, cognitive walkthroughs, ethical stress-testing and scenario-based narratives to validate coherence and responsible design.
